The either-or, reimagined.

One control, twenty ways. We're replacing the small ember pill that picks Sunset vs Always on with something bigger, more custom, more artful — biased toward Apple Liquid Glass and macOS-native idioms so it still belongs in Tahoe Settings. Every concept below is live (click it), sits in the real Schedule context, and uses the exact app palette. Pick directions to pursue; we'll port the winner to SwiftUI.

Where to look first — research picks

  1. Choice tiles (Family C) — two glass cards that show the choice. Strongest all-rounder; mirrors macOS's own picker.
  2. Big segmented, lit-glass thumb (A1) — the baseline grown up. Safe, unmistakably native.
  3. Sky / sun-track toggle (B3 / B-family) — a glassy sun slides horizon→noon; the metaphor IS the control.

The key insight

macOS's own Appearance → Auto already ships this exact either-or — "follow the sun" vs "stay put" — as three preview tiles, and ties Auto to sunset/sunrise.

So the tile-as-mini-scene idiom (Family C) is both maximally native and maximally on-theme. Selection moment to aim for: a matched-geometry slide + a Liquid-Glass ember bloom on the warm ease. Avoid Dribbble clichés (stars, clouds, ticking clock hands).

Motion & finish notes

A quick pass through the design-motion-principles lenses (Emil Kowalski · timing/easing/naturalness; Jakub Krehel · interaction feedback; Jhey Tompkins · creative craft) — things to sanity-check before we commit.

Flags & feel-checks

  • Emil · A3 "Living glyph" runs a slow 14s infinite sun rotation while "Always on" is selected. Ambient continuous motion is usually a smell — it's slow and reads as "holds steady," and it's killed under reduced-motion, but consider swapping for a one-shot bloom-then-settle.
  • Jakub · B5 "Cinematic porthole" and D4/D5 drag the sun with pointer events + rAF easing. Logic is verified headlessly, but the drag feel (momentum, snap-to-nearer-end) needs a real hands-on check — that's where these win or lose.
  • Emil · Selection should resolve in ~140ms on the warm ease (the app's signature). Watch that the bolder concepts (B/D) don't over-animate — the choice must feel instant and certain, with the delight as garnish, not a gate.
  • Jhey · The Liquid-Glass ember bloom at the touch point on selection is the signature moment worth standardizing across whichever direction wins.
  • All · Every concept keeps selected text as dark --indigo on the gold fill (contrast verified), real radio/switch/slider semantics, focus rings, and a reduced-motion path. The native floor is solid.
