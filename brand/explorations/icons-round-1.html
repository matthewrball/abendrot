Icon Refinement · Round 1 · 3-3-1 strategy (§21.4)

Nine takes on the Sunset-arc direction across three families — pure glyph, glass-pebble squircle, abstract orb — each rendered on the dark glossy squircle at 512 / 128 / 32px. Provisional. The founder picks the family; we then refine the winner to a full .icns ramp.

A

Pure glyph — the arc as a precise mark

Minimal, template-friendly, scales to the menu bar with zero loss. The horizon line + half-sun read as "evening" instantly. Strongest at 32px; risks feeling austere at 512px without gloss/depth.

Recommended convergence · 1 of the 9

Converge on B3 — the glossy arc with reflections.

It keeps the poetry of A3 (the half-sun and its two fading water-reflections — the most "Abendrot" of the nine) while gaining the Tahoe-native glass depth of family B (top sheen, warm drop-glow, dimensional disc). Critically, it degrades perfectly: the reflections and sheen fall away at 32px and you are left with the clean A1 half-sun + horizon, which is exactly the menu-bar template (see menubar-template.html). One mark, three jobs: app icon (B3, glossy), Finder/Dock mid-sizes (B3 simplified), menu bar (A1 glyph). Honest note: the 32px tiles above are deliberately drawn from the shared A1 glyph template, not auto-downscaled from the 512px art — so "degrades perfectly" is the design decision these mockups demonstrate (one template, drawn per size), not a measured result of shrinking the glossy icon. The real proof comes when the chosen master is built into the full .icns ramp and checked on-device.

If the founder wants more abstract / ownable, the fallback is C2 (setting orb) — it collapses to the same arc glyph, so the template is unaffected either way. The two families share a menu-bar identity, which de-risks the pick.
