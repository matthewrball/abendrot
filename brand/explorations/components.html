06

Reveal True Color — the one place white appears

The signature. Hold the hotkey and a pure-white veil lifts (a spring, not a fade — .interactiveSpring in-app, §21.3). It is the only #FFFFFF in the entire system, so accuracy reads as a deliberate, physical event.

True color
Hold to see true color
The veil lifts from the right — pure white, spring-eased — then settles back to warm on release.
Token discipline: --reveal-true-white (#FFFFFF) is the only place pure white is permitted. Body text is #ECE8F4; control chrome that would normally be white — the toggle knob and slider knob — uses off-white --cream #F7ECD9 instead; the wordmark gradient tops out at warm --accent-hi. Nothing but the reveal veil is pure white, so accuracy reads as a genuine, deliberate event.
Contrast (WCAG AA): the ember accent --accent #FFAB5C is a fill / large-element color only — on cream it scores ~1.5:1, well under AA's 4.5:1 for normal text. Even --accent-deep only clears AA-large (3:1) on the deeper creams. For ember-colored small text on cream (captions, links, labels) use the dedicated --accent-ink-on-cream #9C4310, which clears AA-normal (≥4.5:1) on every cream surface in the light ramp. Twilight (dark) grounds are unaffected — body text #ECE8F4 on --indigo already passes comfortably.
07

Making the glass feel "wet" (engineering notes)

Per §21.3, the gap between "looks like Tahoe" and "is Tahoe" is specular tracking + variable-thickness blur. The web mockups above approximate both; the app uses native APIs.

Specular tracking (web): each .glass hosts a .spec layer; JS sets --mx/--my to the pointer position so a soft screen-blend highlight follows the cursor. Hover any surface above to see it. In-app: a thin gradient overlay on the NSGlassEffectView whose center binds to NSEvent.mouseLocation, clamped + eased.

Variable-thickness blur (web): the .glass::before ring masks a tighter inset shadow toward the rim, implying the glass is thicker at the edge (a lens). In-app: layer a second, higher-radius backdrop near the bezel, or use the system's lensing parameters on the glass material.

Respect the user: all of this is suppressed under Reduce Motion (no specular animation, instant state) and Reduce Transparency (solid ember, §05). Motion budget stays ~100–150ms; the only "big" moment is the reveal spring.
