# Abendrot marketing docs — index & hierarchy

This folder holds Abendrot's marketing material. There is **one canonical claims source**, and
everything else derives from it. Read this index first to know what each file is for and which one
wins when they disagree.

> **The rule:** cite nothing in any campaign, page, post, or snippet that is not grounded in
> [`evidence-base.md`](evidence-base.md). If a claim isn't traceable there, don't publish it — add it
> to the "Needs founder/legal review" list in [`messaging-and-campaigns.md`](messaging-and-campaigns.md)
> instead.

## Hierarchy (most authoritative first)

1. **[`evidence-base.md`](evidence-base.md) — CANONICAL.** The single source of truth for every
   science claim: 12 approved headline claims with §13/FTC-safe wording, exact spectral levels, the
   24-paper verified citation library, the red-light narrative, the full **DO-NOT-CLAIM** list, and
   the four binding guardrails. Adversarially verified. **Do not edit casually — this is the spec.**
2. **[`messaging-and-campaigns.md`](messaging-and-campaigns.md) — READY CAMPAIGN COPY.** Site copy,
   taglines, brand voice, social drafts, SEO keywords/meta, and AEO Q&A. Every science claim here
   already traces to the evidence base. Use this for site / social / SEO / AEO.
3. **[`science-snippets.md`](science-snippets.md) — APP / LANDING "THE SCIENCE" SNIPPETS.** Short,
   hedged, general-wellness snippets for the app's "The Science" panel, the landing page, and the
   README. **Every snippet must trace to `evidence-base.md`.** Items marked `[VERIFY]` use sources
   not in the canonical library and must be reconciled before going live.
4. **[`launch/`](launch/) — PER-CHANNEL POST DRAFTS.** [`show-hn.md`](launch/show-hn.md),

   [`timeline.md`](launch/timeline.md). Drafts only — all external posts are founder-gated. Every
   claim must comply with the guardrails below.

## The four binding guardrails (from `evidence-base.md`, §13 + FTC)

1. **Couple warmth with brightness.** The app warms but does not dim; the circadian "dose" is driven
   by intensity. Always pair "removes blue light" with **"and lower your brightness."**
2. **Red is circadian-SPARING / low-melanopic, NEVER red-light-therapy / photobiomodulation.** This
   is an imagery guardrail too, not just wording.
3. **Never juxtapose a product claim with sleep-latency / melatonin data** (it implies a sleep
   benefit our own cited RCT — Duraccio 2021 — failed to find). Keep product claim and citation in
   separate breaths.
4. **Precise spectral/CCT numbers are illustrative standard-curve modeling, not measured product
   output.** Never say "Abendrot reduces your melanopic exposure by X%."

For the full banned-claims list, see **[`evidence-base.md` → "⛔ DO NOT CLAIM"](evidence-base.md)**.
